# DomainCERTin — Verified Domains for the Authenticated Internet

## What Is DomainCERTin

DomainCERTin is BlockCertsAI’s **authenticated domain registry and routing layer** — the naming system of the authenticated internet.

It replaces Web2 DNS with **identity-verified, fraud-proof domains** that are cryptographically bound to the owner’s **Secure Virtual Space (SVS)**.

These are not just URLs.
They are **trusted gateways** into applications, SmartSites™, services, and digital assets across the BCAI ecosystem.

---

## Why DNS Had to Be Replaced

Web2 DNS was built for speed, not truth.

As a result, it enables:
- anonymous domain ownership
- phishing and spoofed sites
- typosquatting
- domain hijacking
- fake portals impersonating real institutions

Security tools attempt to detect these problems *after* damage occurs.
DomainCERTin prevents them **at registration**.

---

## Core Capabilities

### KYC-Backed Domain Ownership
Every DomainCERTin address is registered through **Proof of Authentication (PoA)**.

- no anonymous ownership  
- no burner domains  
- no bot-generated sites  

Ownership is tied to a verified real-world identity.

---

### Immutable and Fraud-Proof
Domain ownership is permanently anchored on-chain.

This structurally eliminates:
- phishing
- impersonation
- typosquatting
- domain hijacking

A domain cannot be faked because the owner cannot be faked.

---

### Native Launch Points for Digital Services
A DomainCERTin address can directly launch:

- SmartSites™
- decentralized applications
- enterprise portals
- government services
- health record access
- voting systems
- NFTCERTin ticketing
- private SVS workspaces

The domain is not a pointer to a server.
It is a **cryptographic entry point** into authenticated infrastructure.

---

### Secure by Default
Every domain resolves through the owner’s **Secure Virtual Space (SVS)**.

- no centralized hosting providers
- no exposed servers
- no data scraping
- no DNS middlemen

Security is inherited from the substrate, not layered on.

---

### Permissioned and Token-Gated Access
Domains can enforce access rules by design:

- INK-based utility gating
- verified voter or patient identity
- enterprise role-based permissions
- ADT-driven business logic

Access is enforced *before* interaction begins.
