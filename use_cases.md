## 1. Coordination Without Trust

Most large-scale coordination today depends on **assumed trust**.

Organizations rely on contracts, audits, credentials, platforms, and human
oversight to coordinate action between parties that do not fully trust one
another. These mechanisms exist because execution itself is not enforceable.

As a result:
- verification is repeated
- enforcement is delayed
- disputes are common
- coordination costs scale faster than outcomes

Under protocol-enforced identity and execution, these assumptions disappear.

When every participant is authenticated at genesis, every action is authorized
before execution, and every state transition is attributable, coordination no
longer depends on trust between parties.

It depends on **the substrate**.

Agreements become executable constraints rather than promises.
Roles become enforceable permissions rather than titles.
Responsibility becomes provable rather than inferred.

In this environment:
- multi-party coordination can occur without escrow
- workflows span organizations without shared infrastructure
- delegation does not require surrendering control
- enforcement does not require escalation

Coordination becomes **a property of the system**, not a function of belief.

Trust is no longer negotiated.
It is unnecessary.

## 2. Commerce Without Custody

Commerce today is built on **custody**.

Assets are held by platforms.  
Funds are controlled by intermediaries.  
Execution depends on trusted third parties to settle, reconcile, and enforce outcomes.

Because identity and authority are not enforceable at the protocol layer, custody
becomes the mechanism through which risk is managed.

This creates structural consequences:
- users surrender control to transact
- platforms become systemic points of failure
- compliance is enforced through aggregation
- fraud is absorbed as a cost of doing business

Under protocol-enforced identity and execution, custody is no longer required.

When ownership, authorization, and execution are bound to verified identity,
economic activity does not need to pass through a central holder of assets.

Assets remain in sovereign control.  
Permissions replace possession.  
Execution replaces settlement.

In this environment:
- transactions do not require escrow
- marketplaces do not need to hold funds
- exchanges do not need to custody assets
- compliance does not require aggregation

Economic activity occurs **in place**, authenticated and attributable at the moment
of execution.

Commerce no longer depends on who holds assets.
It depends on **who is authorized to act**.

Custody becomes optional.
In many cases, it disappears entirely.
