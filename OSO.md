# OSO — Open Structure Organization

## Definition

An Open Structure Organization (OSO) is an entity that operates on user-controlled infrastructure where identity, data, AI, and execution are owned and governed at the protocol layer.

OSOs do not rely on rented software platforms.  
They operate within infrastructure they control.

---

## Core Principle

The organization MUST operate inside infrastructure where:

- identity is authenticated
- data is user-owned
- execution is permissioned and verifiable
- AI operates within enforced constraints

Ownership replaces dependency.

---

## Problem Statement

Traditional organizations:

- rely on fragmented SaaS platforms
- store data across multiple third-party systems
- duplicate identity and access controls across tools
- incur recurring subscription costs
- manage risk through layered security rather than structural control

This creates:

- operational fragmentation
- data exposure
- dependency on external providers
- escalating cost and complexity

---

## OSO Model

An OSO consolidates all operations into a unified environment:

- identity
- communication
- data storage
- workflows
- AI execution
- transaction processing

All functions operate within a single governed infrastructure.

---

## Identity Requirement

All participants MUST:

- operate under verified identity
- act within defined authority
- have permissions explicitly granted

Anonymous or unverified participation is not permitted.

---

## Data Ownership

All data MUST:

- reside within a Secure Virtual Space (SVS)
- be controlled by its owner
- not be exposed outside defined permissions

No external platform may assume ownership or control of organizational data.

---

## Execution Model

All actions MUST:

- be authorized prior to execution
- occur within defined process flows
- be verifiable and auditable

No implicit trust or post-hoc validation is allowed.

---

## AI Integration

AI within an OSO MUST:

- operate under AATT constraints
- act on behalf of verified identity
- access only permitted data
- execute only within authorized scope

AI is an extension of authority, not an independent actor.

---

## Economic Model

OSOs operate on a metered usage model:

- cost aligns to actual usage
- no subscription dependency is required
- transactions are authenticated and verifiable

---

## What OSOs Eliminate

OSOs remove:

- fragmented SaaS dependencies
- duplicated identity systems
- uncontrolled data exposure
- subscription-based infrastructure lock-in
- platform-level control over operations

## Summary

An OSO is defined by:

- infrastructure ownership
- identity-based execution

- enforced trust boundaries

Not inside someone else’s.
